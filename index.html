Game Rules

X is always red, O is always blue, and tied games are recorded as CAT. In two-player games, Player 1 is X by default in round 1 and Player 2 is O by default. The starting player alternates after every completed round.

Traditional Tic Tac Toe

  • Play on a standard 3x3 board.
  • Players alternate turns placing X and O in empty cells.
  • The first player to make three in a row horizontally, vertically, or diagonally wins.
  • If every cell fills and nobody has three in a row, the result is CAT.
  • Computer mode uses minimax perfect play. The human goes first in round 1, then the starter alternates.

Infinite Tic Tac Toe

  • Play on a 3x3 board, but each player may keep only three active pieces.
  • When a player places a fourth piece, that player's oldest piece disappears immediately.
  • The current player's oldest active piece appears faded before the move.
  • A player wins by creating a normal three-in-a-row line using currently active pieces.
  • Computer mode simulates future piece removals with minimax-style search and heuristics.

Ultimate Tic Tac Toe

  • Play on a 3x3 grid of smaller 3x3 boards.
  • The cell a player chooses determines which small board the next player must play in.
  • If the required small board is already won or full, the next player may choose any open board.
  • Win a small board by making three in a row inside it.
  • Win the overall game by winning three small boards in a row. If all small boards close with no overall winner, the result is CAT.

Tic-Tac-Ku

  • Uses the same nine-board layout and move-routing rules as Ultimate Tic Tac Toe.
  • The cell a player chooses sends the next player to the matching small board.
  • If the required small board is closed, any open small board may be chosen.
  • Win small boards by making three in a row inside them.
  • The first player to win at least five small boards wins. Those five boards do not need to be in a row.
